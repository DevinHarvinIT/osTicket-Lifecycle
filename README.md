## Overview

This lab documents a full ticket lifecycle in osTicket, from end user intake through triage, escalation, resolution, and closure. The goal is to demonstrate professional help desk workflow, documentation habits, SLA handling, and team based ticket ownership in a realistic support environment.

---

## Environment and Tools Used

- osTicket
- Azure Cloud Virtual Machine   
- Windows 10 Pro  
- Microsoft Remote Desktop  

---

## Skills Demonstrated.

- Ticket intake and documentation  
- Incident triage and prioritization  
- SLA selection and escalation workflow  
- Team reassignment and department handling  
- Ticket correspondence documentation  
- Resolution confirmation and closure

## **Step 2 – Agent Access: IT Support Logs In and Opens the Ticket**

![Step 2 – Agent Login](images/S4.png)

![Step 2 – Ticket Opened](images/S5.png)

The IT support agent authenticates into the osTicket agent panel and opens the newly created ticket to begin review and initial triage.

---

<br>

---

## **Step 3 – Triage: Priority, SLA, and Help Topic Updates**

![Step 3 – Priority Update](images/S6.png)

![Step 3 – SLA Update](images/S7.png)

![Step 3 – Help Topic Update](images/S8.png)

The ticket is triaged to determine business impact and urgency. Priority is elevated to Emergency, the SLA is updated to Sev A, and the help topic is changed to Business Critical Outage to ensure the issue follows the appropriate escalation path.

---

<br>

---

## **Step 4 – Escalation: Assigned Ticket to SysAdmin**

![Step 4 – Assign Ticket](images/S9.png)

![Step 4 – SysAdmin Assignment](images/S10.png)

![Step 4 – Department Context](images/S11.png)

The ticket is assigned to a SysAdmin team member for advanced investigation and remediation. Ownership changes are documented to ensure continuity and accountability as the issue progresses.

---

<br>

---

## **Step 5 – Ticket Correspondence and History**

![Step 5 – Ticket History](images/S13.png)

Throughout the ticket lifecycle, correspondence and actions are documented. Maintaining updates across the duration of the ticket ensures the end user and IT teams share a clear understanding of progress, ownership, and resolution details.

---

<br>

---

## **Step 6 – Resolution: Restore Service, Confirm Fix, and Close Ticket**

![Step 6 – Resolution Notes](images/S12.png)

![Step 6 – Ticket Closed](images/S15.png)

The issue is resolved by restoring service and validating functionality. Resolution details are documented, confirmation is obtained with the end user, and the ticket is marked resolved and closed to complete the lifecycle.
